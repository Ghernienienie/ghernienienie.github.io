My First Few Days 

Fresh Start

Life

After 10 years of Education, here we go again. Like any other students, my first goal was to make friends. I was advised to choose the "right ones". Though that was not my priority, mine instead was getting the muscle memory of my legs ensure I get to the right venue. What a challenge considering the vastness of Singapore Polytechnic, moreover navigating towards a school that's relatively further than the others.

Lessons

First day of school was a difference of night and day from Primary and Secondary school. In poly, I had observed that your attention and attendance is purely your own choice. Myths such as polytechnic classrooms being rowdy were busted instantly in one day.

Independence is truly a virtue here.

My First Few Weeks 

Friends

Life

Friends came like surprise gifts. The first one was Dominique, who used the effective card: Bro you want to makan together?. Under the same sun, more friends tagged along. Like adventurers, we conquered each of the six food courts in SP. Each day, we flipped the page of our unique stories with an open spirit. Rants and trivias were always looked forward to. Especially in a novel environment like poly.

Having someone to talk to is as essential as breathing here.

Lessons

Like I mentioned, myths were busted instantly within a day, and then, even more within a week. I had imagined my homework list to be as short as it can be, until an avalanche of to-do list buried me with no mercy. In poly, flipped learning is practiced - which is basically the end of the spectrum from the traditional classroom learning. No one to remind you, no one to force you, no one to scold you.

Here, you are your own discipline master.

My First Few Months 

Cope

Life

Poly can be fast or slow to unique individuals. To me, no doubt, it certainly is fast. 6 weeks came by in a blink of an eye. Perhaps I enjoyed myself throughout the month. There is a saying, Time is fast when you're enjoying... If that is so, then my circle of friends are to thank for.
Balance is crucial in poly. Keeping the balance of life and play in equilibrium is no easy shot. It requires constant commitment.

That to me, as a result from experience in my first month in Singapore Polytechnic.. is what poly is all about.

Lessons

I used to feel like a fish out of water whenever I feel that I am engaged in a war against deadlines; but here, I got accustomed to the fact that it is often inevitable to be in close contact with deadlines and pushing tasks. Productivity became my favorite word and friends and self-motivation became my drive.
